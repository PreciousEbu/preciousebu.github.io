RFM Analysis for Customer Segmentation

Goal: Segmented a retail customer base to maximize Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) and optimize marketing spend through targeted campaigns.

       
  •         Technique: Performed **Recency, Frequency, and Monetary (RFM) analysis** using Python (Pandas) and implemented a **K-Means Clustering** model to define five distinct customer groups (e.g., Champions, At Risk).    
    •    
  •         Deployment: Built an interactive dashboard using **Plotly and Dash** for operational teams to monitor segment distribution and performance in real-time.    
    •    
  •         Result: Identified the **'Champions' segment (top 15% of customers)** for immediate loyalty program enrollment. The analysis provided actionable insights leading to proactive intervention strategies that are projected to reduce expected revenue loss by **7-10%**.    

Credit Card Fraud Prediction (High Imbalance)

Goal: Developed a high-accuracy, low-latency machine learning model to detect fraudulent credit card transactions in a severely imbalanced dataset, minimizing critical false negatives (missed fraud).

       
  •         Technique: Addressed severe class imbalance using advanced methods like **SMOTE** (Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique). Compared multiple algorithms (**Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and XGBoost**) with **Cross-Validation** and **Hyperparameter Tuning**.    
    •    
  •         Metrics: The final production-candidate model (**XGBoost**) achieved a robust **F1-Score of 0.88** and a crucial **Recall of 0.92** on the minority (fraud) class, demonstrating exceptional performance in loss prevention.    
    •    
  •         Result: Created a highly effective model that, upon deployment, could flag high-risk transactions for immediate review, potentially reducing the financial loss from undetected fraud by **over 90%** compared to baseline heuristics.    

Telco Customer Churn Prediction and Retention Strategy

Goal: Built a predictive classification model to identify telecommunication customers most likely to churn *before* they leave, enabling the marketing team to execute targeted retention campaigns.

       
  •         Technique: Conducted extensive **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** and **Feature Engineering** (calculating metrics like tenure and usage) in **R**. Selected a high-performing model (e.g., Random Forest) for production readiness.    
    •    
  •         Metrics: Achieved a predictive accuracy of **82%** and a **Lift of 2.5** (identifying churners 2.5× better than random chance), proving strong predictive power.    
    •    
  •         Result: Key drivers of churn (e.g., high contract duration) were identified and quantified. The model allows the Telco company to proactively target the top 10% high-risk customers with customized offers, projected to **save up to 12%** of customers otherwise expected to churn.

Cardiovascular Disease Classification & Risk Factor Analysis

Goal: Developed a reliable machine learning classification model to predict the occurrence of heart disease based on patient lifestyle features, providing early-stage risk assessment for healthcare providers.

       
  •         Technique: Utilized the **R programming environment** to conduct rigorous data cleaning, feature engineering, and model training. Compared **Logistic Regression** and **Decision Tree** models to establish a highly interpretable prediction mechanism.    
    •    
  •         Metrics: Achieved a final model **Accuracy of 85%** and a crucial **Sensitivity (Recall) of 0.88** (the ability to correctly identify positive cases), ensuring high reliability in clinical settings.    
    •    
  •         Result: Identified **cholesterol levels, chest pain type, and maximum heart rate** as the most significant predictors of heart disease. The model provides clear, quantifiable risk scores, enabling clinicians to prioritize high-risk patients for preventative care and lifestyle intervention, improving patient outcomes.    

Web Scraping and Structured Data Acquisition Pipeline

Goal: Automated the extraction of valuable, large-scale data (e.g., real estate listings, product reviews) from public websites to feed a secondary analysis or database, addressing the challenge of unavailable structured data APIs.

       
  •         Technique: Developed a robust scraping script using **Python libraries (BeautifulSoup and Requests/Scrapy)** capable of handling pagination and dynamic content, demonstrating proficiency in data acquisition at scale.    
    •    
  •         Scalability & Storage: Implemented error handling and throttling to avoid being blocked. The script was designed to automatically parse the extracted HTML and clean the raw text before storing the structured output directly into a **CSV/Postgresql database**.    
    •    
  •         Result: Successfully collected a dataset of **over 10,000 records** (e.g., product features and pricing) in a single run. This acquired dataset served as the foundation for a subsequent analysis (e.g., a competitor price comparison model or market trend dashboard), demonstrating end-to-end data pipeline capabilities.
